CLIENT GOALS
System needs to allow drivers to rate shippers and receivers
based on their personal experience.
System must have a keyword filter to flag and take down any
inappropriate posts or reviews.
System needs to allow users to view their past trips and require
users to rate a trip in an allotted amount of time.
System needs to be versatile to be adapted and used by other
trucking companies.
DESIGN QUESTIONS
How might we design a website that improves the
efficiency of drivers’ experiences with shippers and
receivers?

USER RESEARCH
Our user research strategy for Load Dock 411 primarily consisted of
information sessions and competitive research. To begin, we interviewed
our client to ask questions about what they were looking for in the design
and information about the users we were designing for. Essential basic
information was given on the company and their values, background
information on user demographics, and the client goals. After this, we
were able to assemble with our fellow teammates to discuss
the various components of our system. Afterwards we compiled the
information and research we had received and began working on
constructing the system our client had described.
We began to research design inspiration and our competition to see
where the market currently stood. Right away we found a few applications
and websites that had similar functions and features that we were hoping
to include in our design. We were intrigued with Trucker Path, Dock411,
Uber Freight, Jitterbug Smart2, and Granpad. There are elements from
each that we were inspired by but they all had the same overall sleek and
simple interface that our users would be able to easily navigate. This is
extremely important to our design because our primary users are older
and not technology savvy.
Because we want to design an app our users love, we began to
research design that is user friendly for the elderly. (continued on next
page)
Requirements Analysis | Page 7
USER RESEARCH
We chose large bolded sans serifs that are easy to read, made the main
navigation in a single, linear menu, and kept the colors simple and not
too overwhelming. This was inspired by Jitterbug Smart2 and Granpad.
Both applications use larger buttons and have the navigation right there
for the user to find so they do not become lost in the pages. The fonts are
large and simple to read, especially on a smart phone or tablet.
When it came to competitors, one example is Uber Freight. They
created a rating feature that allows drivers to rate facilities on a scale of 1
to 5, with the option to leave a written review. It also includes the option to
confirm if there is a restroom, onsite scale, food, or rest areas close. We
think this is a great idea to incorporate into our system as some carriers
may not include features that a driver would want to know about before
hand.
After compiling all of our research from our client, the takeaways from
our inspiration and competition, and outside research we were able
to compile a list of our design requirements. There are five main parts to
the system: a login page, a user home page, a search page, a page to
rate a shipper or receiver, and a product page. Each of these pages will
have specific features to ensure that they fit the client’s goals and the
user’s needs to the best of our team’s ability.

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS
A user home page where drivers can review

A search page where users can search and create dock

USER STORIES
For our user stories we relied heavily on the information our clients provided
for us and outside research conducted on our target audience through
facebook groups. We based our personas on the types of individuals that
would be using this website.
Michael Anderson is a public user of the website and would have access to
everything except for the administrative side. Michael is based off of the
typical driver our client described to us. Because he would be the primary
user of our system, it is important his goals are met and included into our
design.
As a truck driver, I want to search and find the average wait and load
time at a location before I go so I can make the most of my hours on the
clock.
As someone who rarely uses technology outside of work, I want a simple
interface that is easy to navigate.
Tina Emerson is also a public user of the website that would use the system
as needed. Tina is based off the users that drive less frequently, but are still
important to consider in our design. She represents the users that were
interested in being able to share their experience at a carrier as well as
attractions nearby.
As a driver, I want to be able to share my experience at a shipper or
receiver so I can feel connected to a community of fellow drivers that
value my input.
Trevor Sampson is a private user of the website that would have access to
administrative controls. He would be able to upload the upcoming loads for
his drivers as well as take down reviews that are untrue or outdated. He
represents the client and future companies that adopt this system.
As a manager, I want to be able to make sure the reviews left are
accurate so my drivers can plan their trips to be efficient.
